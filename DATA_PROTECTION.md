# Data Protection for Research Participants

Thank you for your willingness to support the research project "SenseUI" in its empirical work. Your insights and contributions will help give shape to SenseUI and will be documented for a master’s thesis in Human-Computer Interaction at the University of Siegen (Germany).

## Legal basis and data collection

The following is an explanation of how the data is determined:

1. The personal data of the co-desginers (you) and, if applicable, the companies, such as names and contact details, will be stored for research activities and used exclusively for the purpose of analysis or for contacting by the project. The project staff undertake to treat the data confidentially and not to pass it on to third parties.

2. Your data will be pseudonymized. Pseudonymization means the processing of personal data in such a manner that the personal data can no longer be attributed to a specific data subject without the use of additional information, as such additional information is kept separately and is subject to technical and organizational measures to ensure that the personal data cannot be attributed to an identified or identifiable natural person.

3. Pseudonymized personal data of co-researchers will be stored for up to ten years after the end of the project, unless the co-researcher expressly requests deletion. Anonymized data will also be stored for more than ten years.

4. The information generated by the empirical work and the data collected about the workplace in the context of the project are stored for scientific purposes, analyzed as part of the research work and used for publications. The analysis of the results is, of course, anonymous, so that the data material does not allow any conclusions to be drawn about individual persons. The same rules apply to scientific publications.

5. The project staff will contact the co-researchers several times during the project in order to obtain feedback from the participants. Furthermore, questions relating specifically to the research can be clarified. In this way, a successful course of study can be guaranteed.

Your data will be processed exclusively within the framework of the project and will not be passed on to third parties.

## Use of AI API

SenseUI uses an AI API (pending) to analyze your code and provide feedback. Here's what that means:

### What gets sent to the AI:

- The HTML and CSS code of the website you use with SenseUI
- Screenshot of the page
- Your questions and requests

### What happens to that data:

- The API service processes it to generate feedback
- Data may be retained according to the AI service's privacy policy
- We do not use your code to train or improve the AI model
- Your data is not shared with third parties beyond the AI service

**Important:** We are still evaluating which AI service to use. Before you share code, check back here for which service we've chosen and review their privacy policy.

**Your responsibility:**
- Do not share confidential, proprietary, or sensitive information through SenseUI
- If your code contains secrets, API keys, or private information, remove them first
- Check your employer's policies about using third-party AI services


## Rights of data subjects according to the GDPR 

You are entitled to data subject rights. To exercise these rights, it is sufficient to notify the controller in text form (letter, e-mail or fax). However, exercising your rights will only have an effect if the processed data allows a natural person to be identified. 

1. Revocation (Art. 7 para. 3 GDPR): The provision of personal data is voluntary. Consent to the processing of personal data can be revoked at any time in accordance with Art. 7 para. 3 GDPR with the consequence that the personal data of the person concerned will no longer be processed.

2. Right to information (Art. 15 GDPR): Data subjects have the right to request information about the personal data processed about them and the possible recipients of this data at any time. They are entitled to a response within one month of receipt of the request for information.

3. Right to rectification, erasure and restriction (Art. 16–18 GDPR): Data subjects can request the rectification, erasure or restriction of processing of their personal data from the project managers at any time.

4. Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR): Data subjects may request that the controller transmit their personal data to them in a machine-readable format. Alternatively, they may request the direct transfer of the personal data provided by you to another controller, insofar as this is possible. Portability only applies to personal data that is processed by automated means.

5. Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR): If the data subject has grounds relating to their particular situation, they may object to the processing of their personal data at any time. This data will then no longer be processed unless the project controller can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing which override the interests, rights and freedoms of the data subject or for the establishment, exercise or defense of legal claims.

6. Right to lodge a complaint (Art. 77 GDPR): Data subjects can contact the data protection officer of the University of Siegen (Mr. Sebastian J. Zimmermann; Sebastian.Zimmermann@uni-siegen.de), Ms. Padmé Nalbach and, in the event of a complaint under Art. 77 GDPR, the competent supervisory authority for data protection at any time. The competent supervisory authority is: Ministry of Culture and Science of the State of North Rhine-Westphalia (Völklinger Str. 49, 40221 Düsseldorf).

Data security also has a high priority with regard to the rights of data subjects, which is why the assertion of data subject rights is only possible after the data subject has been identified beyond doubt. Please also note that due to the research nature of the project, the rights provided for in Art. 15, 16, 18 and 21 GDPR are limited to the extent that these rights are likely to render impossible or seriously impair the achievement of the research purpose and the restriction is necessary for the fulfillment of the research or statistical purposes. Furthermore, the right to information pursuant to Art. 15 GDPR does not exist if the data is required for the purposes of scientific research and the provision of information would require a disproportionate effort (Section 28 (2) BDSG).
